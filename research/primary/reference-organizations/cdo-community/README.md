# CDO Community Sources

This folder collects official and organization-owned primary materials for the
Cyber Domain Ontology (CDO) community and its closely related ontology
surfaces, especially Unified Cyber Ontology (UCO) and CASE.

The intent here is not to adopt CDO, UCO, or CASE wholesale as ACES authoring
syntax. It is to keep the primary materials close at hand while ACES defines a
shared concept model and decides where cyber-domain concepts should come from,
where ACES-native extensions are needed, and how manifests, contracts, and SDL
surfaces should bind to shared meaning instead of reusing strings loosely.

## Why These Sources Matter

Taken together, these sources support a cleaner concept-model story for ACES:

- UCO is a strong candidate semantic spine for cyber-domain concepts such as
  assets, identities, relationships, observables, actions or events, and tools
  or artifacts.
- ACES still needs native extensions for experiment and runtime concerns that
  do not naturally belong to classic cyber ontology, including scenarios,
  tasks, runs, studies, manifests, realization disclosures, provenance,
  evidence expectations, and time or apparatus concerns.
- Using a shared concept authority helps SDL, processor manifests, backend
  manifests, provenance, and reporting surfaces point at the same meaning
  rather than merely repeating the same string.
